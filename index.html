TIMELINE JUMPERS

Easter Hunt 2026 - Save the Future

Welcome, Timeline Jumpers!

You are Brynja, Patrik, and Joakim - three dimension-jumpers recruited by the Temporal Resistance.

THE TEAM:

Brynja (19) - Strategic leader with a sharp mind
Patrik (18) - Athletic and fearless
Joakim (17) - Creative problem solver

Your mission: travel to a dystopian future timeline where a totalitarian regime has banned everything deemed "unhealthy." The society is suffocating. There is no music. No art. No dancing. No real food - only pills and supplements.

Your Quest: Find the 4 hidden Fragments of Joy scattered across Løsjeskogen. These artifacts will prove to future world leaders that joy, creativity, and pleasure are essential to being human.

But beware: you don't yet know who started this prohibition. The final location will reveal the truth...

LOCATION 1 OF 4

The Silent Grove

In this timeline, all musical instruments were destroyed 50 years ago. The last violin, the last piano, the last drum - all ash.

But you've found a place where someone left behind a secret message, encoded in the language of music itself.

Decode the Message:

You discover a carved message at the ancient oak tree. Someone encoded a word using a Caesar cipher (each letter is shifted forward by 3 positions):

P X V L F

Shift each letter BACK 3 positions in the alphabet to decode the hidden word.

(Hint: Think of the alphabet. If P is at position 16, what is 3 positions back?)

LOCATION 2 OF 4

The Library Stone

This timeline burned all books. Every novel, every poem, every painting was incinerated. But someone hid a literary riddle here, carved in stone, waiting for someone who still remembers words matter.

Solve the Ancient Riddle:

"I have cities but no houses. I have forests but no trees. I have water but no fish. What am I?"

This object was once cherished by artists, writers, and dreamers everywhere.

LOCATION 3 OF 4

The Amphitheater Ruins

This ancient stone circle was once a place of celebration, movement, and joy. Now it's overgrown and forgotten.

On one stone, a sequence of numbers has been etched - a logic puzzle left by someone who remembers how to dance between logic and emotion.

Find the Pattern:

On the stone, you find this sequence of numbers. Each one follows a specific pattern:

2, 5, 10, 17, 26, ?

What is the next number in the sequence?

(Hint: Look at the differences between consecutive numbers. Do they follow a pattern?)

LOCATION 4 OF 4 - FINAL

The Garden of Ashes

This was once a thriving garden where people grew vegetables, fruits, and herbs. Now it's buried under ash and restriction pills.

In the center, you find a locked box. There's a final puzzle - and when you solve it, you'll discover who started this prohibition...

The Final Challenge:

Four friends in the old world loved different foods. Using the clues below, determine who loved which food:

1. The person who loved spicy food was not named James.
2. Sarah did not love sweet food.
3. The person who loved salty food sat next to the person who loved sweet food.
4. Marcus loved the opposite flavor intensity as the person who loved umami.
5. Emma loved something with only 4 letters.

What is the 4-letter food that Emma loved?
